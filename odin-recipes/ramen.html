Tonkotsu Ramen

A japanese classic that everyone wants to taste

This recipe may not quite hit the level of Japanese ramen making. People have devoted their whole lives to mastering the art. The principal ingredients are: Broth, Noodles, Pork and Eggs.

Ingredients

Tonkotsu Ramen
  • 8 cups of tonkotsu prok broth
  • 12 oz dried noddles
  • 4 large boiled eggs
  • 2 oz mushrooms
  • thinly sliced green onions
Chashu pork belly
  • Pork belly
  • Soy sauce
  • Sake
  • Mirin
  • Sugar
  • Garlic cloves
  • Green onions
Soy bacon tare
  • Slices of bacon
  • Soy sauce
  • Sake
  • Shiro miso
Miso tare
  • Shiro miso
  • Sake
  • Mirin
  • Salt

Steps

Chasu pork belly
  1. Combine the soy, sake, mirin, sugar, garlic and green onions in a ziploc bag large enough to hold the pork belly.
  2. Sous vide the pork for 10-11 hours at 170F.
  3. Remove pork from the ziploc bag. Discard the bag and marinade.
  4. Let the chashu pork belly cool completely.
  5. Slice the chasu pork
Soy bacon tare
  1. Combine all the ingredients in a small sauce pan. Simmer at the lowest setting for about an hour.
  2. Remove the bacon
Miso tare
  1. Combine all ingredients in a small sauce pan and simmer at the lowest setting for about 5 minutes.
Medium boiled eggs
  1. Boil large eggs for 6 minutes 30 seconds.
  2. Submerge the eggs in cold or ice water to chill.
  3. Peel. Cut in half right before you serve your tonkotsu ramen.
Asemble the tonkotsu ramen
  1. Boil the ramen noodles in plenty of water.
  2. Cook the mushrooms along side the noodles.
  3. Gently fry the chashu pork in a non-stick skillet until lightly browned.
  4. Place 1/4 of whichever tare you are using in the bottom of four bowls.
  5. Ladle in about 1/2 cup of the tonkotsu broth into each of the bowls and stir to mix.
  6. Add the noodles. Pour in another 1 1/2 cups of the tonkotsu broth per bowl.
  7. Top with the egg, mushrooms, pork and green onions.
